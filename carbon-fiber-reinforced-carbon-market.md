<p><ul><li>Chemical Vapor Deposition</li><li>Liquid Impregnation Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Carbon Fiber-Reinforced Carbon (CFRC) market is primarily segmented into two key production types: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation Process. CVD involves depositing carbon-rich gases onto a substrate to form a composite material, offering high purity and precision. In contrast, the Liquid Impregnation Process involves saturating carbon fibers with liquid resins that cure to create strong, lightweight structures. Both methods cater to various industries, including aerospace and automotive, due to their enhanced strength and thermal resistance.</p></p>
