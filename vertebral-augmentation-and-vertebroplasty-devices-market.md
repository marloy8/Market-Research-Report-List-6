<p><p>Vertebral augmentation encompasses minimally invasive procedures aimed at stabilizing vertebrae affected by fractures, commonly due to osteoporosis. It primarily includes vertebroplasty and kyphoplasty. Vertebroplasty involves injecting medical cement into fractured vertebrae to provide structural support and pain relief. In contrast, kyphoplasty involves inflating a balloon in the vertebra before cement injection to restore height and reduce deformity. The market for these devices is driven by the rising incidence of spinal fractures, increased awareness, and advancements in minimally invasive surgical techniques.</p></p>
