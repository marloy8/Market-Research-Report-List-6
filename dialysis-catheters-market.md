<p><p>Dialysis catheters are commonly used for hemodialysis treatment in end-stage kidney disease patients. Long-term hemodialysis catheters are designed for extended use and are typically inserted into larger veins for regular dialysis sessions. Short-term hemodialysis catheters, on the other hand, are intended for temporary use and are usually inserted into smaller veins for short-term dialysis treatments. Both types of catheters play a crucial role in facilitating the removal and return of blood during the dialysis process.</p></p>
