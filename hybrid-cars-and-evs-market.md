<p><p>Hybrid cars and electric vehicles (EVs) are categorized into three types: HEV, PHEV, and EV. HEVs (Hybrid Electric Vehicles) combine internal combustion engines with electric propulsion for improved fuel efficiency. PHEVs (Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles) offer both a gasoline engine and a larger battery that can be charged externally, allowing for longer electric-only ranges. EVs (Battery Electric Vehicles) operate solely on electric power, requiring no gasoline. Each type serves distinct consumer needs and contributes to reducing carbon emissions.</p></p>
