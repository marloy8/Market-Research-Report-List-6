<p><p>The coating thinners market encompasses various solvent types used to reduce the viscosity of coatings for better application. Mineral spirits are commonly used for oil-based paints, while turpentine is derived from pine resin and often used in artistic applications. Naphtha acts as an effective degreaser, and toluene is known for its strong solvent properties. Methyl Ethyl Ketone (MEK) is favored for its quick evaporation rate, and Dimethylformamide (DMF) improves adhesion. 2-Butoxyethanol serves as a versatile solvent for cleaning and painting, and other specialty thinners cater to specific industrial needs.</p></p>
