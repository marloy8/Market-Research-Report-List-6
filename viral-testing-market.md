<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Viral Testing Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Direct Fluorescent Antibody (DFA) Test</li><li>Immunochromatographic Assay</li><li>RT-PCR-Based Test</li><li>Agglutination Assay</li><li>Flow-Through Assay</li><li>Solid-Phase Assay</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The viral testing market includes several key types:</p><p>1. **Direct Fluorescent Antibody (DFA) Test**: This involves staining samples with fluorescent antibodies to visualize viral antigens under a microscope.</p><p>2. **Immunochromatographic Assay**: A rapid test that uses antibodies to detect viral proteins, often in a simple strip format.</p><p>3. **RT-PCR-Based Test**: A molecular technique that amplifies viral RNA for accurate detection, widely used for diagnosing viral infections.</p><p>4. **Agglutination Assay**: This method detects viruses by observing the clumping of particles in the presence of viral antigens.</p><p>5. **Flow-Through Assay**: A technique where samples move through a membrane, allowing for quick detection of viral components.</p><p>6. **Solid-Phase Assay**: Utilizes immobilized antibodies to capture and detect viral particles, enhancing sensitivity and specificity.</p></p>
