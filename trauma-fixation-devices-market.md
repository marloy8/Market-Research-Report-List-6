<p><p>The trauma fixation devices market comprises internal and external fixation devices used to stabilize fractures and promote healing. Internal fixation devices, such as plates, screws, and rods, are surgically implanted within the body to provide stable support for broken bones. In contrast, external fixation devices are attached to the outside of the body, utilizing pins or screws that penetrate the skin and hold the fractured bone in place. Both types are essential for effective orthopedic trauma management and recovery.</p></p>
