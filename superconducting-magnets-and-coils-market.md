<p><p>Superconducting magnets and coils are classified primarily into two market types: AC (alternating current) and DC (direct current). AC superconducting coils are utilized in applications involving fluctuating magnetic fields, such as in MRI machines and particle accelerators, where rapid changes are required. In contrast, DC superconducting magnets provide stable, continuous magnetic fields for applications like fusion research and magnetic energy storage. Both types leverage the unique properties of superconductors, enabling efficient energy use and enhanced performance in a range of technologies.</p></p>
